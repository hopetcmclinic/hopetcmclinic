<p>
Fu's Subcutaneous Needling (FSN) is a contemporary acupuncture technique that has been gaining recognition for its unique approach to treating various health conditions. Invented by Dr. Fu Zhonghua, a Chinese doctor, in 1996, FSN diverges from traditional acupuncture by targeting the subcutaneous layer of the skin. This article explores the intricacies of FSN, shedding light on its safety, efficacy, and ability to provide immediate pain relief for many patients.
</p>
<br>

<h2 class="subtitle">What is FSN?</h2>
<p>
    FSN, also known as Fu's Acupuncture, involves the insertion of specially designed needles into the subcutaneous layer of the skin. Unlike conventional acupuncture, which primarily targets acupuncture points along meridians, FSN focuses on stimulating the subcutaneous tissue, including nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue. The needles used in FSN are ultra-thin and flexible, allowing for precise and gentle insertion.
    With the characteristics of much less pain for the patient and an instant effect of pain relif, patients quite often describe FSN as a "magic needle with unbelievable effects".
</p>
<br>

<h2 class="subtitle">Is FSN Safe?</h2>
<p>Safety is paramount in any therapeutic modality, and FSN is no exception. One of the key advantages of FSN is its minimally invasive nature. The ultra-thin needles used in FSN reduce the risk of tissue damage and discomfort, making it a safer alternative for individuals with needle phobias or sensitivities. Moreover, FSN is performed by trained practitioners who adhere to stringent hygiene and sterilization protocols, further ensuring patient safety.</p>
<br>

<h2 class="subtitle">Why Stimulate the Subcutaneous Layer</h2>
<p>
The subcutaneous layer, located just beneath the skin's surface, plays a crucial role in maintaining overall health and well-being. It houses a dense network of nerves, blood vessels, and lymphatic channels that regulate sensory perception, circulation, and immune function. By targeting the subcutaneous layer, FSN aims to modulate these physiological processes, promoting balance and harmony within the body. Additionally, stimulating the subcutaneous tissue can elicit localized and systemic therapeutic effects, making FSN a versatile treatment option for a wide range of conditions.
In the treatment of pain symptoms, especially acute pain, FSN can instantly relieve the pain after inserting the needle or shortly after finishing the manipulation.
</p>
<br>

<h2 class="subtitle">Differences Between Traditional Acupuncture and FSN</h2>
<p>
While both traditional acupuncture and FSN share the common goal of restoring health and alleviating symptoms, they differ significantly in their techniques and targets. Traditional acupuncture relies on the insertion of needles at specific acupuncture points along meridians to regulate the flow of Qi (vital energy) and restore balance within the body. In contrast, FSN bypasses the traditional acupuncture points, instead targeting the subcutaneous layer to directly influence the underlying physiological processes. This innovative approach allows FSN to address complex conditions that may not respond adequately to traditional acupuncture alone.
</p>
