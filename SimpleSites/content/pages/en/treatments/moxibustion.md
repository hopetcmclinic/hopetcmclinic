<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Ancient Warmth for Healing</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
Moxibustion, often referred to as "moxa," traces its origins back thousands of years in China. It involves the burning of mugwort, a herb with purported healing properties, to stimulate specific points on the body. At the core of moxibustion lies the fundamental principle of TCM â€“ the balance and flow of Qi.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
Moxibustion aims to restore harmony and promote healing by stimulating the flow of Qi and enhancing the body's innate ability to heal itself. It is especially renowned for dispelling cold and dampness from the body.
</p>

<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Techniques</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
Practitioners of moxibustion employ various techniques:
</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-2">
    <li><strong>Direct moxibustion:</strong> Placing a small cone of moxa directly on the skin at acupuncture points (removed before burning the skin).</li>
    <li><strong>Indirect moxibustion:</strong> Burning moxa on top of acupuncture needles or using a moxa stick over specific regions of the body to create a gentle, penetrating warmth.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Benefits</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
Moxibustion is believed to offer a myriad of therapeutic effects, including relieving pain and inflammation, boosting the immune system, and improving circulation. It is commonly used to treat conditions such as arthritis, digestive disorders, menstrual irregularities, and respiratory ailments.
</p>
