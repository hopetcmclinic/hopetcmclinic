<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Introduction</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
At Hope TCM Clinic, we often describe cupping as a "reverse massage." While a typical massage pushes down into the muscles to relieve tension, cupping uses suction to pull the skin, fascia, and muscles upward. This negative pressure is what makes cupping so unique and effective.
</p>
<p class="mb-6">
By creating space within the tissue layers, cupping encourages fresh blood flow to areas of stagnation, loosens tight muscles, and sedates the nervous system. It is an ancient therapy that feels surprisingly modernâ€”providing a deep sense of release that is hard to achieve with other methods.
</p>

<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Types of Cupping</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
We use several different cupping techniques depending on your specific needs and comfort level:
</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-2">
    <li><strong>Fire Cupping:</strong> The traditional method where a flame is briefly inserted into a glass cup to create a vacuum before placing it on the skin. The warmth from the fire adds a soothing element to the therapy.</li>
    <li><strong>Pump Cupping:</strong> Uses plastic cups with a manual suction pump. This allows for very precise control over the amount of pressure used, making it great for sensitive areas.</li>
    <li><strong>Sliding Cupping:</strong> Oil is applied to the skin first, allowing the cups to glide over large muscle groups (like the back). It feels like a deep tissue massage and is excellent for myofascial release.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">Benefits of Cupping</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
Patients mostly seek cupping for pain relief, but its benefits extend far beyond that:
</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-2">

<h3 class="subtitle text-2xl font-serif text-primary mb-4">The "Marks" (Sha)</h3>
<p class="mb-6">
One of the most distinctive features of cupping is the circular marks it leaves behind, known in Chinese Medicine as "Sha." It is important to clarify that <strong>these are not bruises</strong>. A bruise is caused by impact trauma breaking capillaries; Sha is the result of suction drawing toxins, cellular waste, and stagnant blood ("dead blood cells") from deep within the tissue to the surface where the lymphatic system can flush them away.
</p>
<p class="mb-6">
The colour of the marks tells a story about what is happening inside your body:
</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-2">
    <li><strong>Light Red:</strong> Indicates mild stagnation or recent injury.</li>
    <li><strong>Dark Red / Purple:</strong> Suggests long-standing stagnation, chronic pain, or accumulation of toxins.</li>
    <li><strong>Pale / No Mark:</strong> Can indicate vitality deficiency or poor circulation.</li>
</ul>
<p class="mb-6">
These marks are temporary and typically fade within <strong>2 to 7 days</strong>. They are rarely painful, though they may look intense!
</p>
